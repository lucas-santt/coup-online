Statutes of the Bureau

You are a ranking officer of a Ministry within a single-party state under total observation. Deceive, bluff, and discredit rival officers to seize control. Eliminate the influence of every other player: the last officer standing wins.

Influence

Your two face-down cards are your standing within the Ministries. Only you can see them. When you lose an influence, you choose which card to reveal, and it stays face up and visible to everyone from then on. Lose both, and you're removed from the Bureau's registry.

Taking a Turn

On your turn you must choose exactly one action, you cannot pass. If you begin your turn with 10 or more coins, that action must be a Coup. Once you declare an action, other players get a chance to block or challenge it before it resolves. If nobody does, it succeeds automatically.

General Actions

ActionEffect
IncomeTake 1 coin from the Treasury.

CoupPay 7 coins to force another player to lose one influence. Cannot be blocked or challenged. Mandatory once you hold 10+ coins.

Character Actions

Claiming a character action means claiming to hold that card. You never have to reveal it unless challenged.

CharacterActionEffect
Duke (Ministry of Finance)TaxTake 3 coins from the Treasury.
Assassin (the Underground)AssassinatePay 3 coins; target loses one influence unless blocked by a claimed Contessa.
Captain (Ministry of Defense)ExtortTake 2 coins from another player (or their last coin, if they only have 1). Can be blocked by a claimed Ambassador or Captain.
Ambassador (Ministry of Foreign Affairs)ExchangeDraw 2 random cards, then return 2 to the deck, keeping the same number of influences.

Challenges

Any character action or block can be challenged by any player, whether or not they're the target. The challenged player must then reveal the claimed character:

  • If they have it: the challenger loses one influence. The revealed card is shuffled back into the deck and its owner draws a fresh one, so their hand isn't exposed.
  • If they don't: they lose one influence, and the action or block fails (any coins spent on it are still lost).

Note the double-loss trap: failing a challenge against an Assassin (or a bluffed Contessa block) costs you one influence for the failed challenge, then another for the assassination itself.

Reformation adds religion to the Bureau. Every player is either Catholic or Protestant, shown on a Religion card in front of them, alternating around the table at the start of a match. A Reformation match also swaps the Ambassador out for the Inquisitor.

Religion Restriction

You cannot Coup, Assassinate, Extort, or block another player's Foreign Aid if they share your religion, unless every remaining player shares the same religion.

Additional Actions

ActionEffect
ConversionPay 1 coin to switch your own religion, or 2 coins to switch another player's. No character claim needed, cannot be blocked or challenged.
CorruptionClaim not to hold the Duke and take everything from the Asylum pool. Anyone may challenge: if you actually hold the Duke, admit it, return the coins, and lose an influence; if you don't, reveal a card as normal for a lost challenge.

The Inquisitor

In a Reformation match, the Inquisitor replaces the Ambassador entirely. Its owner picks one of two actions per turn (never both):

ActionEffect
ExchangeDraw 1 random card, then return 1, keeping the same number of influences.
InvestigatePrivately view one of an opponent's face-down cards. You may force them to swap it for a fresh random card, or let them keep it.

As a counteraction, a claimed Inquisitor can also block Extortion, just like the Ambassador.
